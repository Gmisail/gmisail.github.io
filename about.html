About Me

I was born in November of 2000, and sometime from then to 2010 I caught the programming bug. I started out by tinkering in iWeb, Apple's website designing application. Feeling restricted, I eventually began figuring out what makes websites tick; this of course was HTML, CSS, and Javascript. After hours upon hours of copy-pasting, watching YouTube videos, and reading countless articles, I finally began to get the hang of programming. This eventually led to me making Minecraft mods, websites, and command line applications (such as The Nice Project, which is what this website is based off of!)

Many years later, I am studying Computer Science at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute in Troy, NY. Although I no longer make Minecraft mods and silly websites in iWeb, I still never stop tinkering and learning new things. However, over the years, I started to gravitate towards some sectors of Computer Science over others, such as:

  • Software Development
  • Compiler / Interpreter Design
  • Game Design
  • Full Stack Development

I am not sure where my future takes me, but no matter what, I know that I will always be programming!
